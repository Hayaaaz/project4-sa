1. Model Weight Sizing  
Before doing the storage math, I looked at the two model sizes used in the course: 13B and 70B. 
The 13B model is the realistic choice for a small school AI estate because it fits comfortably 
on local storage and is commonly deployed for internal RAG systems. The 70B model is included 
only for comparison, to show how much larger the storage footprint becomes at higher parameter 
counts. For the actual estate sizing, I use the 13B model.

Quantization chosen: INT4 (0.5 bytes per weight)

13B model:
Params: 13,000,000,000
Bytes per weight: 0.5
Formula: 13,000,000,000 × 0.5 = 6,500,000,000 bytes
= 6.5 GB on disk

70B model:
Params: 70,000,000,000
Bytes per weight: 0.5
Formula: 70,000,000,000 × 0.5 = 35,000,000,000 bytes
= 35 GB on disk
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

2. Vector Database Sizing  
The vector store holds 5 million embeddings, each with 1536 dimensions.  
I used halfvec (2 bytes per dimension) because it’s the recommended precision
for pgvector and halves storage while maintaining good recall.

- Raw vectors:  
  5,000,000 × 1536 × 2 bytes  
  = 15.36 GB

- HNSW index overhead (~10%):
  15.36 GB × 0.10  
  = 1.536 GB

-Total vector store: 
  15.36 GB + 1.536 GB  
  ≈ 16.9 GB
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

3. Dataset Sizing  

RAG Corpus  
I assumed the system serves as an internal knowledge base (policies, FAQs, procedures, tickets).  
- 10,000 documents  
- 200 KB each  
- Total ≈ **2 GB**

Fine‑Tuning Dataset  
I assumed a small curated dataset of Q&A pairs.  
- 500 pairs  
- 50 KB each  
- Total ≈ 25 MB
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

4. Total Estate Size  
Here is the combined storage footprint:

- 13B INT4 model weights: 6.5 GB  
- Vector database: 16.9 GB  
- RAG corpus: 2 GB  
- Fine‑tuning dataset: 25 MB  

Total:  
6.5 + 16.9 + 2 + 0.025 = 25.425 GB ≈ 25.5 GB
=====================================================================================
This is what we are buying: ~25.5 GB of storage capacity for the school’s AI estate.
=====================================================================================

5. Tiering Table  
My (lsblk)  output shows (/dev/sda2) is rotational (ROTA=1), meaning it’s a spinning HDD with low IOPS.  
Cold and archived data fit naturally here.  
Hot-tier assets would normally live on SSD/NVMe in production.


Asset                     | Tier     | Reason
---------------------------------------------------------------
Model weights             | Hot      | Used live; needs fast access
Vector index (HNSW)       | Hot      | Real-time similarity search
Raw vectors               | Warm     | Accessed often but not per-query
RAG corpus                | Cold     | Read occasionally; low IOPS is fine
Fine-tuning dataset       | Archive  | Rarely accessed; long-term storage
Logs / scratch            | Cold     | Low IOPS; temporary data
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
